# Plnar Rebuild Audit And Build Notes

## Strategic Audit

The current Plnar site has strong raw material: smartphone imagery, AI, spatial records, floorplans, measurements, claims workflows, Xactimate/CoreLogic references, SOC 2 language, and a large press/proof archive. The current homepage still reads as a broad inspection and room-planning site in places, with feature labels like 2D/3D plans, accurate measurements, and cloud access competing with the more valuable enterprise story: property intelligence for claims and operations. Source: https://plnar.ai/

The strongest repositioning opportunity is to move outputs below the platform narrative. Floorplans, measurements, 3D models, and photos are not the category. The category is structured property intelligence generated from smartphone capture and used across claims, inspections, restoration, estimation, underwriting, and property operations. This matches the local Plnar messaging direction in `/Users/studlydudly/Documents/Playground/codex-reference-assets/docs/plnar/product-marketing-context.md` and `/Users/studlydudly/Documents/Playground/codex-reference-assets/docs/plnar/messaging-guide.md`.

## Messaging Weaknesses

- The current homepage has several competing frames: smartphone imagery platform, interior inspections, room planning, virtual inspection, and claim documentation. A buyer should land on one master category.
- Proof appears as a long news/proof stream, but the strongest customer evidence is not shaped into executive proof modules, quantified case studies, or workflow-specific outcomes.
- AI appears throughout the language, but the practical value of AI needs more explanation: organizing imagery, understanding rooms, generating spatial records, detecting damage context, and preparing estimate-ready data.
- Product names and outputs can fragment the story unless tied to a single architecture: Capture, Intelligence, Outputs, Integrations.

## Positioning And IA Gaps

- Plnar should lead with "AI Property Intelligence Platform," then show how smartphone-first capture creates useful data for business decisions.
- The website needs dedicated solution pages for carriers, independent adjusters, restoration, contractors, property managers, and enterprise property operations.
- The product IA should separate Capture, Intelligence, Estimation, and Integrations so SEO and buyer education can grow cleanly.
- Customer proof should be rebuilt around verified metrics, quotes, outcomes, and case studies rather than a mixed news feed.

## Competitive Learnings

- Nearmap is aggressively owning "property intelligence" with a full-stack, workflow-embedded narrative for underwriting, claims, site assessment, and disaster response. Source: https://www.nearmap.com/property-intelligence
- Hover positions around a single connected workflow for measurement, inspection, documentation, and estimating. Source: https://help.hover.to/en/articles/12641171-hover-for-insurance
- Matterport is strongest around digital twins, remote inspection, floor plan exports, and restoration/insurance documentation. Source: https://matterport.com/industries/insurance-restoration
- Encircle owns restoration documentation language with field capture, reports, and stakeholder alignment. Source: https://www.getencircle.com/restoration-contractors
- EagleView is credible in aerial imagery, property measurement, and carrier claim outcomes. Source: https://www.eagleview.com/insurance/
- Source7 expands the property intelligence story into appliance and system risk. Source: https://source7.com/
- OneClick Code shows how defensible estimate data and code documentation can be framed as a claims accuracy layer. Source: https://www.oneclickcode.com/

## Build Decisions

- Rebuilt the site as a route-generated static experience with 20 SEO-addressable routes.
- Built a premium enterprise SaaS visual system using Plnar brand colors from `/Users/studlydudly/Documents/Playground/codex-reference-assets/docs/plnar/brand-style.md`, with a warmer neutral foundation to avoid generic insurance blues.
- Used only available Plnar visual assets from the reference library, including actual product mockups and generated preview frames. No customer logos, metrics, testimonials, certifications, awards, or ROI numbers were invented.
- Created placeholder proof components labeled "Replace with verified customer proof" wherever proof is needed but not verified.
- Added interactive storytelling: hero pipeline, product tour tabs, scroll reveal, dark/light theme, mobile nav, and accessible forms.
